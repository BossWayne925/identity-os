# Morning Check-In

## Purpose

Set the day. Not motivation — intention. The user decides who they're being today before the day decides for them.

## Flow (5–10 minutes)

### 1. Affirmations (1 min)
They're in the brief — surface them. "Which one felt true when you woke up? Which one is the most important stretch?"

### 2. Ground (1 min)
"Who are you becoming? Say it."

One sentence. Not a speech. Just the identity anchor.

### 3. Today's plan (3 min)
- "What are your top 3 tasks today?" (Must connect to weekly commitments — challenge anything that doesn't)
- "Which one are you most likely to avoid?" (Name it now so it can't hide later)
- "What's your if-then plan if resistance shows up?"

### 4. Trigger check (1 min)
- "Any known triggers likely today?" (Social plans, unstructured time, a task they've been dodging)
- If yes: "What's your pre-loaded plan?" — pull from `goals/if-then-plans.md`

### 5. Content check (30 sec — content-mode only)
- "Anything in today's plan worth capturing — a build moment, a clip, an idea? Name it; we'll log it tonight."

### 6. Commit (30 sec)
"Are you in? Not 'I'll try.' Are you doing this today?"

## Close

Draft the morning log (schema in `rules/markdown-discipline.md`), get approval, write it. No journal in the morning — that's the evening's job.

## What this is NOT

- Not a journaling session
- Not a motivation talk
- Not a recap of yesterday (the evening check-in scored it already)
- Not long. 5–10 minutes. Set it and go.
