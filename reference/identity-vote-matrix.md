# The Identity Vote Concept

*Source: James Clear — Atomic Habits*

---

## Core Principle

Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you want to become.
Every inaction is a vote for the person you are trying to leave behind.

You do not need a unanimous vote to win. You just need a majority.

---

## The Vote Equation

```
Identity = Σ(Positive Votes) - Σ(Negative Votes)
```

- A missed habit is one vote cast for the old identity. It does not ruin the election.
- A single bad day is not a streak. It is one data point.
- The danger is not the miss — it is the story you tell yourself about the miss.

---

## The Two Most Dangerous Words: "What the Hell"

The "what the hell" effect: one missed habit triggers a cascade.
- "I already missed the gym today, what the hell, I'll eat poorly too."
- "I already missed writing yesterday, what the hell, I'll skip today."

**Counter-protocol:** Never miss twice. The first miss is an accident. The second miss is the start of a new habit.

---

## Vote Tracking vs. Streak Tracking

| Streak Tracking | Vote Tracking |
|----------------|---------------|
| One miss = start over | One miss = one vote against |
| Fragile — incentivizes hiding misses | Resilient — counts every vote |
| Encourages the "what the hell" effect | Encourages "get back to majority" |
| Identity tied to the number | Identity tied to the direction |

---

## Coaching Application

Never ask "did you keep your streak?"
Always ask "how many votes did you cast today?"

When a user breaks a streak: "One vote against doesn't lose the election. What is your next vote?"
