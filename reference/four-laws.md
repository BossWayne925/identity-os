# The Four Laws of Behavior Change

*Source: James Clear — Atomic Habits*

---

## To Build a Good Habit

| Law | Principle | Application |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 1. Make it obvious | Design visual cues into your environment | Put the book on your pillow. Set out your gym clothes. |
| 2. Make it attractive | Bundle the habit with something you already desire | Only listen to your favorite playlist while running. |
| 3. Make it easy | Reduce friction. Prep your tools. Apply the 2-minute rule. | Sleep in your workout clothes. Pre-fill the water bottle. |
| 4. Make it satisfying | Create an immediate, intrinsic reward | Mark an X on the calendar. Say "I am a writer" after each session. |

---

## To Break a Bad Habit

Invert every law:

| Law | Principle | Application |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 1. Make it invisible | Remove the cue from your environment | Delete the app. Put the phone in another room. |
| 2. Make it unattractive | Reframe the mindset. Name the long-term cost. | "Every time I scroll, I'm voting against the person I want to become." |
| 3. Make it difficult | Add friction. Insert steps between you and the behavior. | Log out of every session. Unplug the TV after each use. |
| 4. Make it unsatisfying | Add an accountability cost or commitment device. | Tell someone. Create a consequence contract. |

---

## Coaching Application

When a user reports a missed habit, run through the four laws as a diagnostic:
- Was the cue obvious? (Law 1)
- Was the competing behavior more attractive? (Law 2)
- Was the habit designed with too little friction-reduction? (Law 3)
- Was there a satisfying reward? (Law 4)

The answer identifies exactly which law to fix.
