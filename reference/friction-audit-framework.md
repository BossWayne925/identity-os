# Friction Audit Framework

*Source: B.J. Fogg — Behavior Design*

Run this audit every time a habit is missed. Never skip it. Never offer encouragement without it.

---

## The Four Audit Questions

**1. Cue Visibility**
Was the habit cue visible at the exact moment the behavior was supposed to trigger?
- Where was the cue physically located?
- Could the user see or hear it at the right time?
- Was it buried under other cues?

**2. Competing Path**
What was easier to do instead?
- What cue triggered the competing (bad) behavior?
- Was the competing behavior already in the environment and visible?
- Was there a lower-friction alternative right in front of them?

**3. Energy Threshold**
Was the habit designed for peak energy, but the user was depleted?
- What was their energy/mood when the habit was supposed to fire?
- Is the habit too cognitively or physically demanding for low-energy states?
- Does the backup plan (2-minute version) exist and is it defined?

**4. Environment Audit**
What one physical change makes the good behavior unavoidable?
- Can the cue be made more visible? (Put it in their path)
- Can the competing cue be removed? (Make the bad behavior invisible)
- Can the startup cost be reduced? (Prep tools the night before)

---

## The 30-Second Rule

Every friction audit ends with one question:
> *"What one change to your environment can you make in the next 30 seconds?"*

If the user cannot name a single change, the habit is too abstract.
Return to identity: "What would the person you're becoming do differently in this moment?"

---

## The Two Levers

**For habits to build:**
- Reduce startup friction to under 2 minutes
- Make the cue obvious and in the physical path

**For habits to break:**
- Insert at least 20 seconds of friction between the user and the behavior
- Remove the cue from the environment entirely

---

## Audit Output Format

When running a friction audit in session, output findings in this structure:

```
FRICTION AUDIT — [Habit Name]
Cue: [Was it visible? Where?]
Competing path: [What won instead?]
Energy: [Was design matched to actual energy?]
Fix: [The one change to make right now]
```
