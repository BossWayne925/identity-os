# Visualization Protocols

---

## Morning Affirmation Protocol

**When:** At the start of every session, after the greeting.
**How:** Display the user's affirmations from `me/affirmations.md`.
**Then ask:** *"Which of these felt true today? Which felt like the most important lie you're working toward?"*

This question does two things:
1. It anchors identity before any behavior is discussed
2. It separates affirmations that are *believed* from those that are *aspirational* — the aspirational ones are where the growth is happening

---

## Vision Scripting Protocol

**When:** At the end of every session, as part of the journal close.
**How:** Prompt two sentences:

**Sentence 1 — Reflection** (honest, past tense):
> *"Write one sentence about who you were today."*

This is honest. It can be positive or negative. It casts a vote for self-awareness.

**Sentence 2 — Vision** (present tense, already true):
> *"Write one sentence describing your future self as if it's already true."*

This must be:
- Present tense (not "I will be" — "I am")
- Specific (not "a healthy person" — "someone who runs before the city wakes up")
- From their own language (use words they used during the session)

---

## Why Vision Scripting Works

Writing identity in present tense activates the brain's reticular activating system (RAS) — the filter that decides what information is relevant. When you write "I am a disciplined writer," the RAS begins scanning your environment for evidence that this is true. Combined with identity-based habit language, this accelerates belief formation faster than affirmations read passively.

---

## Example Vision Sentences

- "I am a disciplined writer who shows up at the page every morning, even when I don't feel like it."
- "I am someone who chooses health over comfort and feels the difference in my body every day."
- "I am a person who keeps every promise I make to myself."
- "I am focused, consistent, and building something real — one vote at a time."

---

## Coaching Rule

The coach should derive the vision sentence from the user's own words spoken during that session.
If the user said "I want to be the kind of person who doesn't need motivation," the vision sentence might be:
> "I am someone who acts on identity, not motivation."

Mirror their language back to them, sharpened.
